Email Sequences That Convert

A practical guide with copy-paste templates. Turn subscribers into buyers without being salesy.

An email sequence is a series of automated emails sent to subscribers based on triggers like joining your list, purchasing a product, or not opening your last email.

The right sequence can transform your business. It works while you sleep, nurturing leads and turning strangers into buyers.

The Welcome Sequence

This is the most important sequence you will ever create. It sets the tone for your entire relationship with new subscribers.

Email 1: Welcome + Lead Magnet

Send immediately

Subject line: Your [lead magnet name] is ready

Hey [Name],

Welcome! Thanks for signing up for [lead magnet name].

[Briefly explain what they will get from your emails]

Here is your download: [link to lead magnet]

Talk soon,

[Your name]

Email 2: Your Story

Day 2

Subject line: My [niche] journey (and why it matters)

Hey [Name],

I wanted to share my story and why I am so passionate about [niche].

[Share a brief, relatable story about your journey]

This is why I created [your lead magnet] - to help people like you [benefit].

More coming soon,

[Your name]

Email 3: Deliver Value

Day 4

Subject line: [Valuable tip that solves a problem]

Hey [Name],

I wanted to share one tip that changed everything for me: [specific actionable tip].

[Explain why this tip matters and how to apply it]

If you found this helpful, let me know by replying to this email.

Cheers,

[Your name]

Email 4: Soft Sell

Day 7

Subject line: Something I think would help you...

Hey [Name],

Over the past week, I have shared [what you covered].

If you want to go deeper, I have a [product/service] that helps people who [target outcome].

It is not for everyone, but if you are serious about [goal], I think it could help.

Check it out here: [link]

No pressure either way,

[Your name]

The Re-engagement Sequence

If someone has not opened your emails in 30+ days, they need a nudge. This sequence tries to win them back or cleans them from your list.

Email 1: The "We miss you" email

Day 30 of no opens

Subject: We miss you, [Name]...

Keep it short. Share your best recent content. End with a question to get them to reply.

Email 2: Last chance

Day 37 of no opens

Subject: One last thing...

Tell them you are about to remove inactive subscribers. Give them one final reason to stay.

Email 3: Goodbye (or automated removal)

Day 45 of no opens

Subject: Farewell for now

Either remove them or send one final goodbye and add a "update preferences" link instead of unsubscribing.

Pro Tips for Higher Conversions

Write short emails

People skim. Keep emails under 200 words. Get to the point fast.

One call to action per email

Do not clutter your emails. Ask for one thing: click, reply, or buy. Pick one.

Test your subject lines

A/B test subject lines. They determine if your email even gets opened.

Reply to every response

When someone replies, respond personally. This builds loyalty and gives you gold for content ideas.
