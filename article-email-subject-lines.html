Email Subject Lines That Get Opened

Your subject line determines everything. If it does not get opened, nothing else matters. Here is how to write ones that demand attention.

You have 2 seconds to make someone open your email. That is it. Your subject line, preview text, and sender name are all you have to compete for attention. Here is how to win that battle.

The Anatomy of a High-Performing Subject Line

Great subject lines share these characteristics:

  • Short: Under 50 characters ideal
  • Specific: Numbers and details work
  • Curiosity-driven: Makes them need to know more
  • Personal: Uses their name or situation

Proven Subject Line Formulas

1. The Number Formula

Numbers stand out and add specificity:

  • 7 ways to double your email list
  • I made $3,247 from this one email
  • 3 mistakes killing your conversions

2. The Curiosity Gap

Leave them wanting more:

  • The email trick no one talks about
  • What I learned from my $10K mistake
  • Stop doing this if you want more opens

3. The Personal Angle

Make it about them:

  • Quick question about your [business]
  • Thought of you when I saw this
  • Your competitor is doing this...

4. The Urgency Play

Create time pressure:

  • This expires at midnight
  • Last chance to [benefit]
  • 48 hours only

Subject Lines to Avoid

  • ALL CAPS (looks like spam)
  • Excessive punctuation (!!!, ???)
  • Spam trigger words: FREE, ACT NOW, GUARANTEE
  • Vague: "Newsletter #42" or "Update"
  • Too long: cuts off on mobile

A/B Testing Your Subject Lines

Always be testing. Create variations and measure:

  • Question vs statement
  • With number vs without
  • Personal vs generic
  • Emoji vs no emoji
  • Short vs long

Preview Text Matters

The preview text is your second chance. Use it to expand on your subject line:

Example:

Subject: The mistake everyone makes

Preview: I made this mistake for 2 years. Here is the fix...

Final Thoughts

Your subject line is the gateway to everything else. Spend as much time on it as you do writing the email itself. Test constantly, measure results, and iterate.
