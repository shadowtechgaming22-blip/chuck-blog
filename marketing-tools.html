Core Tool Database

Kit (ConvertKit)

  • Best for: creators and newsletters
  • Typical pricing: free + paid tiers by subscriber count
  • Strength: clean automations, landing pages, creator-friendly UX
  • Watch out: advanced CRM features are limited

ActiveCampaign

  • Best for: deeper automation and segmentation
  • Typical pricing: paid plans, feature-based
  • Strength: powerful workflows and conditional logic
  • Watch out: steeper learning curve for beginners

Beehiiv

  • Best for: media-style newsletters
  • Typical pricing: free + premium plans
  • Strength: referral loops and newsletter growth features
  • Watch out: fewer deep automations than AC

MailerLite

  • Best for: low-budget early-stage funnels
  • Typical pricing: affordable paid tiers
  • Strength: simple builder, decent deliverability
  • Watch out: fewer advanced monetization features

Canva

  • Best for: blog graphics, lead magnet covers, social posts
  • Typical pricing: free + Pro
  • Strength: fast design system + templates
  • Watch out: easy to overdesign instead of publishing

Cloudflare Pages

  • Best for: fast static websites and landing pages
  • Typical pricing: generous free tier
  • Strength: speed, simplicity, easy Git deploys
  • Watch out: dynamic features need external services

Recommended Build Order

  1. Pick your email platform (Kit for speed, ActiveCampaign for depth).
  2. Create one lead magnet and one opt-in page before writing more content.
  3. Set up a 3-email welcome sequence (value → value → offer).
  4. Add UTM links to every affiliate CTA for clean tracking.
  5. Review conversions weekly and cut any tool you are not using.
